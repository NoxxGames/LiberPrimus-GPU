# Research Notes

## Established facts

Liber Primus is a public unsolved puzzle artifact with known solved and unsolved portions. Stage 0A does not reproduce or verify any page solution.

## Community hypotheses

Future work may evaluate classical substitutions, rune alphabets, prime-derived streams, transpositions, and composed transforms. These are catalog entries, not claims.

## Open questions

Key unresolved questions include canonical transcripts, image provenance, rune-table variants, page numbering, normalization policy, and fair null controls.

## Evidence quality levels

Use levels such as raw source, locked transcript, reproducible solved fixture, reviewed hypothesis, generated candidate, and rejected candidate.

## First sources to mirror later

Stage 0B should identify authoritative public source archives, community mirrors, image sources, transcript repositories, and licensing notes before acquisition.

## Solved-page reproduction requirement

Known solved pages must later be reproduced from locked corpus inputs before new search work begins.

## Page-56 prime-minus-one / phi-prime note

Prime-minus-one and phi-prime style hypotheses should be treated as research leads only. They need locked inputs, exact sequence definitions, null controls, and reproducible tests.

## False-positive risk

Short texts, flexible rune mappings, hand-picked transforms, and retrospective scoring create high false-positive risk. The manifest and result schema must make that risk visible.

## Stage 0A research status

No research corpus, solved fixture, or candidate plaintext is generated in Stage 0A.
